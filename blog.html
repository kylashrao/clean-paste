The Complete Guide to Tracking Tokens & Rich-Text Formatting

Published: June 2026 | Reading Time: 3 mins

What is a URL Tracking Parameter?

When you copy a link from major platforms like Amazon, Twitter, or Facebook, the URL is often packed with marketing telemetry strings like ?utm_source=, &fbclid=, or &pf_rd_r=.

These snippets don't help load the webpage. Instead, they act as footprints that track your digital journey, sharing your browsing context across platforms when you send a link to a coworker or friend. Clean-Paste strips away these tokens, letting you share clean, short, canonical links safely.

Why Rich-Text Formatting Breaks Workflows

Copying content directly from software like Microsoft Word, Google Docs, or modern websites carries hidden style layouts, heavy font sizes, or inline HTML code blocks along with it.

Pasting this messy clipboard payload straight into an email client, code editor, or blog CMS often distorts your text formatting. Passing your text through Clean-Paste isolates the pure strings instantly, saving your layouts.
